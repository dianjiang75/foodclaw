# UI Style Research: Dark Bento Editorial for FoodClaw

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Recommendation:** Dark Bento Editorial — the best alternative to our warm organic build
**Target audience:** Health-conscious millennials + Gen Z (22-38)

## Why This Style

- 73% of Gen Z prefer dark mode
- Food photos convert 25-35% better on dark backgrounds
- Bento grid = perfect layout for dish-first discovery (variable card sizes = visual hierarchy)
- Editorial typography adds trust + personality ("curated friend" not "algorithm")
- Polar opposite of warm organic build — gives us two distinct options to A/B test

## Design System

### Colors
- Background: #0A0A0A (near-black)
- Surface: #141414 with #1A1A1A borders
- Primary: #FF6B35 (warm orange — appetite-stimulating)
- Health green: #22C55E (dietary safety)
- Warning red: #EF4444 (allergens)
- Text: #F5F5F5 (off-white) / #9CA3AF (muted)

### Fonts
- Headlines: Space Grotesk Bold 700 (geometric, modern, "informed")
- Body: Inter Regular/Medium (most readable UI font)
- Numbers: Inter Tabular Figures (aligned macros)

### Layout: Bento Grid
- Hero tile (2x2): featured/trending dishes, large photo
- Standard tile (1x1): regular results, square photo
- Wide tile (2x1): editorial picks, photo + description side by side

### Key Principles
- Food photography is the hero — dark bg makes it pop
- Dietary safety badges MUST be prominent (green check, amber warning, red X)
- Glassmorphic search bar (semi-transparent dark glass with backdrop blur)
- Macro badges as pills overlaying bottom of food photos
- Moody food photography style (dark shadows, refined tones)
